STUDY LEAVE AND RETURNS

What is Study Leave?

Study Leave is an approved period during which a teacher is granted permission to pursue further education.

It is intended to improve professional qualifications and enhance service delivery in the education sector.

PHASE 01

ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS FOR STUDY LEAVE WITH PAY

To qualify for study leave with pay, a teacher must:

Minimum Years of Service
  • Have completed at least three (3) years of continuous service after first appointment.
  • Must have served in a deprived/rural area if required by current GES policy guidelines.
Good Conduct and Performance
  • Must have satisfactory annual appraisal reports.
  • Must not have pending disciplinary cases.
  • Must be recommended by the Headteacher and District Director.
Admission into an Accredited Institution
  • Must have gained admission into an accredited tertiary institution such as:
    • University of Education, Winneba
    • University of Cape Coast
    • University of Ghana
  • The course must be relevant to GES needs (e.g., Education, Mathematics, Science, Special Education, etc.).
Bond Agreement
  • Applicant must sign a bond to serve GES after completion of studies.
  • The bond period is usually equal to the duration of the study leave.

PHASE 02

PROCESS OF APPLYING FOR STUDY LEAVE WITH PAY

01

Announcement

GES releases an official circular announcing:

  • Study leave categories
  • Quota for each district/region
  • Approved subject areas
  • Deadline for application
02

Submission of Application

The applicant must submit:

  • Completed study leave application form
  • Admission letter
  • Appointment letter
  • Academic certificates
  • Recent payslip
  • Recommendation letters

Application is submitted through: Headteacher → District Education Office → Regional Education Office → GES Headquarters.

03

Screening and Selection

Applications are screened based on:

  • Years of service
  • Subject area priority
  • Performance record
  • District quota availability
04

Approval and Release Letter

Successful applicants receive:

  • Official study leave approval letter
  • Instructions on reporting after completion

Salary continues during the study period.

CONDITIONS DURING STUDY
LEAVE

1

Teacher must not take up another employment.

2

Must pursue the approved course only.

3

Must complete the programme successfully.

4

Must inform GES of any changes.

PHASE 01

REQUIREMENTS FOR STUDY LEAVE RETURN

The returning officer must submit the following documents for reinstatement:

  • Application for reinstatement
  • Completion letter from institution
  • Academic transcript
  • Certificate (or official result slip)
  • Study leave approval letter copy

PHASE 02

PROCESS OF STUDY LEAVE RETURN

01

Reporting & Verification

Reporting to District Office

Teacher reports to the District Education Office with required documents.

Verification of Documents

GES verifies:

  • Completion of programme
  • Authenticity of certificate
02

Reposting & Salary

Reposting

Teacher is:

  • Reposted to a school (may not necessarily be the former school).
  • Assigned based on subject specialization and vacancy.

Salary Adjustment (If Applicable)

If the new qualification qualifies the teacher for:

  • Upgrading (e.g., Diploma to Degree),
  • Rank promotion,

GES processes salary adjustment accordingly.

Important Notes

  • !
    Failure to return after study leave may lead to:
    • Suspension of salary
    • Legal action to recover paid salaries
  • ! Teachers must serve the bond period after return.
  • ! Study leave without pay follows a slightly different procedure.
The Study Leave Policy of the Ghana Education Service enables teachers to upgrade their qualifications while ensuring continued service to the education sector. The process involves eligibility verification, official approval, and proper reinstatement after completion.
