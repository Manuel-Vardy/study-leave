GEDP Teacher Transfer System

The GEDP Teacher Transfer System is an official administrative and digital system used to process and manage the transfer of teachers from one school, district, or region to another within Ghana.

It helps GES control teacher distribution, ensure fairness, and maintain proper staffing levels in schools.

What is the GEDP Teacher Transfer System?

The GEDP-Teacher Transfer System is a structured process and online platform that allows teachers to:

Apply for transfer from their current post
Request relocation to another district or region
Submit supporting documents electronically
Track the status of their application

The system is fully managed by the GES headquarters together with district and regional education directorates.

Phase 1

Confirm Eligibility for Transfer

Before applying, a teacher must ensure they meet the basic GES transfer conditions:

Basic Conditions:
  • Must have served at least 3 years at the current post (normally required).
  • Must have no pending disciplinary issues.
  • Must have proper documentation supporting the reason for transfer.
Common Acceptable Reasons:
  • Marriage
  • Health condition
  • Family responsibilities
  • Further education
  • Security concerns
  • Spouse relocation

Phase 2

Apply Online on the Transfer Portal

Log into GEDP and navigate to the Transfer Portal to process your transfer. The following information is required:

Information Required:
  • Staff ID
  • Current school
  • Current district/region
  • Preferred district/region
  • Reason for transfer
  • Supporting documents
Apply on the Transfer Portal

Step 2

Application Review

The request moves through several levels of review. Each level may recommend approval or rejection.

1

Headteacher

Initial review and recommendation at the school level.

2

District Education Office

District-level review of the transfer application.

3

Regional Directorate

Regional-level assessment and recommendation.

4

GES Headquarters

Final authority for all transfer decisions.

Step 3

Final Decision

GES makes the final decision based on:

  • Teacher demand in both areas
  • Validity of the reason for transfer
  • Availability of vacancies

Step 4

Issuance of Transfer Letter

If approved, the teacher receives a formal transfer letter indicating:

  • New district or region
  • Reporting date
  • Posting instructions

Final Steps

Release and Assumption of Duty

Once approval is granted, the following steps must be completed:

1

Current district issues a release letter.

2

Teacher reports to new district education office.

3

Teacher receives posting to a school.

4

Teacher signs assumption of duty form.

Types of Transfers in GES

The system allows different types of transfers based on the teacher's circumstances and needs.

Inter-District Transfer

Movement from one district to another within the same region.

Inter-Regional Transfer

Movement from one region of Ghana to another region.

Special Transfer

Transfers based on exceptional circumstances:

  • Medical conditions
  • Security concerns
  • Marriage or family relocation

Important Notes
for Teachers:

  • Transfers are usually processed during official transfer windows announced by GES.
  • Some transfers (e.g., medical or security cases) may be treated as emergency transfers.
  • Teachers must continue teaching until officially released.
